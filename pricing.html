Understanding the Difference

Personal Subscriptions

Designed for individuals using tools for themselves. You have full autonomy over your personal journey. Tools focus on self-support, emotional grounding, and personal growth. No clinical features, analytics, or professional permissions.

Professional Subscriptions

Designed for licensed mental health professionals using tools in clinical practice. Includes both professional-facing and client-facing tools, plus the infrastructure to support therapeutic relationships through client access codes.

Why Separate Paths?

Clear role boundaries protect both practitioners and clients. Personal users maintain complete privacy and autonomy. Professional users have clinical controls and ethical access management. The separation ensures psychological safety for everyone.

Can I Have Both?

Yes. If you're a mental health professional who also wants personal access for your own growth, you can maintain both a professional subscription (for clinical work) and a personal subscription (for yourself). They serve different purposes and maintain clear boundaries.

Common Questions

I'm working with a therapist who uses Leaf2Root. Which subscription do I need?

If your therapist is a Leaf2Root professional subscriber, they'll provide you with a client access code that gives you access to the tools without needing your own subscription. If you want additional personal access beyond what your therapist provides, you can get a personal subscription.

I'm a mental health professional. Can I just get the client subscription?

Client subscriptions are for personal use only and don't include professional tools, client access codes, or clinical integration features. If you're using tools with clients in a therapeutic context, you'll need a professional subscription to maintain proper ethical boundaries and access the full professional toolkit.

Why can't I see prices for both options on the same page?

We intentionally separate personal and professional pricing to respect role boundaries and prevent comparisons that don't reflect the different value and infrastructure each provides. Personal and professional subscriptions serve fundamentally different purposes, and side-by-side pricing can create unhelpful comparisons.
